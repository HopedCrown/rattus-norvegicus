The Norwegian Rat generally has a brown coat and a white underbelly, though the subspecies **domestica** (the Domesticated subspecies) has a grey coat. They typically also have black eyes and 4 'fingers'.
They also live for up to **2** years in the wild, but, under human care, that ***doubles***. 

If you thought that **ant hills** were packed, check out these guys' burrows! They also have **insane** birthrates, so that makes the burrow *more* crowded! 


> P.S. If you want to get the birthrates stuffs, then just go to the Special Adaptation

The Norwegian Brown Rat also weighs approximately 11 ounces (according to the state of Illinois) and has a tail length of 6 to 8.5 inches long. They also have litters of 6 to 12 young, with females capable of 4 to 6 litters a year. They also cause property damage, according to the State of Illinois.
