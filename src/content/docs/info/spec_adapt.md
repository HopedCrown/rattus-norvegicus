*Rattus norvegicus* has its own, unique adaptation. It reproduces in ***very*** large amounts. (Specifically: 6-12 young & 4-6 litters, with young being reproductively mature in 3 months after birth, and young being born 21-23 days **after** birth.)
When a large proportion of these comes to an area, they can quickly outnumber the pesticides in place, and make it harder to eradicate them **by the day**. This is an important adaptation for a **parasitic** species with a diverse diet, as it allows them to overcome some dynamic (movable) limiting factors.
